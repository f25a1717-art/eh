The Bioscience Scenario

In sustainable food security and agro-forestry setups across Malaysia, tracking vegetative biomass generation is a critical metric for predicting land optimization and canopy yield potential.

This application simulates the growth of perennial crop variants on acidic Malaysian soils treated with specialized magnesium-rich structural fertilizers. Biomass volume accumulates slowly during early establishment, enters an exponential vegetative expansion phase, and levels off as it approaches canopy equilibrium.

Mathematical Modeling via Derivatives

To find the instantaneous growth rate of the crop biomass at any given moment, we apply differential calculus to a verified Logistic Growth Function.

1. The Population Function $f(x)$

The total dry biomass weight (grams) over time $x$ (months after transplanting) is given by:

f(x) = A / (1 + b * e^(-c * x))

Constants Used (Realistic Fit):

  • A = 450.0 (Asymptotic Maximum Dry Biomass in grams)
  • b = 18.5 (Initial scaling coefficient)
  • c = 0.25 (Specific biological growth rate factor)

2. The Derivative Function $f'(x)$

By applying the Chain Rule and Quotient Rule, the first derivative representing the instantaneous rate of biomass change (g/month) simplifies to:

f'(x) = (A * b * c * e^(-c * x)) / (1 + b * e^(-c * x))^2
